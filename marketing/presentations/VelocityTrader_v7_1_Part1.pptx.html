PHYSICS-BASED ANALYSIS

Kinematic Physics Model

Core Physics Concepts

Mass (m)
Liquidity depth from Level 2 DOM or synthetic calculation. Higher mass = thicker market, more resistance to movement.
Force (F)
Order flow imbalance: volume delta × price direction. Measures buying/selling pressure.
Velocity (v)
Rate of price change in points per tick/bar. Tracked as average over 3 periods for smoothing.
Acceleration (a)
Change in velocity, normalized using Welford statistics for statistical significance testing.

Key Equations

F = volume × direction
v = Δprice / Δtime
a = Δv, normalized

Physics-Based Regime Detection

Momentum Persistence

Tracks consecutive bars in same direction
Velocity threshold: RL-tunable parameter
TREND requires 5+ persistent bars
PHYSICS IMPLEMENTATION

Market Mass & Force Calculation

Mass Calculation

Live Mode (Level 2 DOM)
When Level 2 data is available:
Aggregate bid/ask volumes across 10 levels
Sum total liquidity: mass = Σ volumes
Represents actual market thickness
Fallback Mode (Synthetic)
When L2 unavailable (backtest or no data):
Calculate from volume-to-price-delta ratio
Apply exponential decay: mass = mass × 0.95 + new × 0.05
Clamped between 10-1000 for stability
Interpretation: High mass = thick book, low mass = thin book

Force Calculation

Force Formula
F = volume_Δ × direction
Where: volume_Δ = current_volume - prev_volume
direction = sign(price_change)
High Force
Strong Pressure
Large volume + clear direction
Low Force
Weak Pressure
Low volume or chop

Velocity Calculation

Raw Velocity

Acceleration & Normalization

Raw Acceleration
a_raw = v_now - v_prev
Welford Normalization
Online algorithm maintains running statistics:
Mean: μₙ = μₙ₋₁ + (xₙ - μₙ₋₁)/n
M2: M2ₙ = M2ₙ₋₁ + (xₙ - μₙ₋₁)(xₙ - μₙ)
Std: σ = √(M2/(n-1))
Z-Score: z = (x - μ)/σ
Benefits: No history storage, O(1) memory, robust to outliers

Regime Identification
